Amortization Calculations

What is Amortization?

Amortizationis the process of breaking a loan down into smaller payments, each containing interest, and principal amount.

Formula for Amortization

Amortization can be calculated using the following formula:

A = P * [i(1+i)y] / [(1+i)y - 1]

  • A: Annual payment
  • P: Loan amount (principal)
  • i: Interest rate (in decimal form, ex. 5% = 0.05)
  • y: Number of years

Steps to Calculate Amortization

  1. Convert the interest rate to decimal format, if it's not already.
  2. Plug the values of P, i, and y into the formula.
  3. Compute the result using a calculator or software.

Example Calculation

Suppose you have a loan of $10,000 at an interest rate of 5% over 5 years. The calculation would look like this:

Parameter Value
Loan Amount (P) $10,000
Interest Rate (i) 5% or 0.05
Number of Years (y) 5
